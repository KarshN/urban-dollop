Scientists mull harmful effects of technological exposure on adolescent brains

By Frank O. Pan, Science Correspondent

A growing body of research suggests that excessive exposure to technology may be subtly altering the neural pathways of adolescent brains, according to a controversial new report released Monday by the Institute for Brain Hygiene (IBH).

"The teenage brain is in a crucial developmental phase," says Dr. Harold Pinsley, lead author of the study. "When you expose it to 14 hours of TikTok, video games, and AI chatbots per day, you might as well be throwing a blender in a library."

"Our preliminary scans show what appears to be a literal flattening of the frontal lobe in some subjects," said Dr. Alina Cho, neuroimaging specialist.

The IBH study tracked over 3,000 teens over the span of four years. Results indicated a significant increase in attention fragmentation, digital dependency, and an inexplicable desire to start podcasting.
